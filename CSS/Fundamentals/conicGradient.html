Understanding Conic Gradients

A unique way to blend colors around a central point.

What is a Conic Gradient?

Unlike linear or radial gradients, a conic-gradient() distributes colors around a central point, along a circular path. Imagine a pie chart or a color wheel â€“ that's how a conic gradient works!

Syntax & Properties:

.element-with-conic-gradient {
    background: conic-gradient(
        [from <angle>]
        [at <position>],
        <color-stop-list>
    );
    /* Example used in this demo: */
    background: conic-gradient(
        from 45deg at 50% 50%,
        red 0%,
        yellow 25%,
        lime 50%,
        blue 75%,
        purple 100%
    );
}
  • from <angle>: Optional. Specifies the starting angle of the gradient. Default is 0deg (top, pointing upwards).
  • at <position>: Optional. Defines the center point of the gradient. Default is center (or 50% 50%).
  • color-stop-list: A comma-separated list of colors and optional stop positions (e.g., red 0%, blue 100%).
  • Colors progress clockwise around the center point.

Conic gradients are perfect for creating color wheels, pie charts, fancy buttons, and other radial designs.
